睡再多也不夠？當心嗜睡症找上門

Heart disease, diabetes, weight gain, even cancer -- they're all risk factors when you can't get enough sleep.

心臟病、糖尿病、體重增加，甚至是癌症——這些都是你睡眠不足時可能導致的危險因子。

For many people, lack of sleep isn't a product of their environment or even their habits, but a medical issue.

對於許多人而言，缺乏睡眠並不是環境或甚至他們習慣的產物，而是醫學方面的問題。

In fact, there are over a hundred different types of sleep disorders.

實際上，睡眠障礙有上百種不同的類型。

The most common is insomnia -- waking up at night or having trouble falling asleep in the first place.

最常見的是失眠，也就是會在夜裡醒來，或是從一開始就難以入眠。

Almost all of us experience some type of insomnia at one point in our lives.

幾乎我們所有人在一生中某個時候都經歷過某種類型的失眠。

But, for more than 10 percent of the population, it is a chronic condition.

不過，有超過百分之十的人口，對他們來說這卻是一種慢性疾病。

Now, for others, there's the opposite problem.

不過，另外有些人遭遇的卻是相反的問題。

Disorders like narcolepsy and hypersomnia mean you're constantly feeling fatigued and sleepy, no matter how much sleep you get.

像是猝睡症與嗜睡症這樣的病，意謂著不論你睡了多少，都還是一直感到疲勞睏倦。

While the conversation about sleep is almost always how to get more of it, is there such a thing as too much sleep?

關於睡眠的討論幾乎總是在於如何獲取更多睡眠，但真的有睡太多這回事嗎？

You know the feeling -- groggy and tired, not fully awake, not fully aware.

你知道那種感覺——昏昏欲睡、疲倦無力、不完全清醒、意識不完全清晰。

We all get this way now and then when we're sleep-deprived.

我們偶爾都會因為睡眠不足而陷入這種狀態。

But, for Danielle Hulshizer, it is a constant way of life.

不過，對於丹妮爾．赫席澤而言，這卻是生活常態。

Sleep isn't a restful thing for us, and you don't wake up feeling energized and ready to go.

對我們而言，睡眠沒辦法帶來休息，醒來之後不會覺得精力充沛、蓄勢待發。

You wake up still feeling just as tired as you did 12 hours before when you laid down to go to sleep in the first place.

你醒來之後，仍然覺得自己就像起初十二個小時前躺下睡覺時那樣的疲累。

For a long time, Danielle's constant craving for sleep was blamed on her busy schedule.

長期以來，丹妮爾持續的嗜睡現象被歸咎於她繁忙的行程。

Danielle was falling asleep frequently and fast, which brings us to our next sleep tidbit.

丹妮爾不但經常睡著，而且很快就入睡，這又可帶到下一個睡眠小知識。

If it takes you less than five minutes to fall asleep at night, you're probably sleep-deprived.

如果你在晚上不到五分鐘即可睡著，那麼你可能有睡眠不足的問題。

Ideally, falling asleep should take 10 to 15 minutes.

理想上，入睡應該要花大約十到十五分鐘的時間。

Fourteen years ago, Danielle signed up for a sleep study and she was diagnosed with idiopathic hypersomnia --

十四年前，丹妮爾加入了一個睡眠研究，結果被診斷出原發性嗜睡症——

a sleep disorder, where the feeling of sleepiness never wears off, even after sleeping all night and taking long naps during the day.

這是一種睡眠障礙，就算睡了一整夜而且在白天又睡了很長的午覺，睏倦的感覺從不曾消失。

It interfered with her education, career, relationships -- her entire life.

這種現象干擾了她的學業、她的工作、她的人際關係——她的人生。

After the diagnosis, the initial treatment plan was stimulant medication to keep Danielle awake.

做出診斷之後，最初的治療方案是以興奮劑類藥物讓丹妮爾保持清醒。

It worked -- at first.

這種方法確實有效——剛開始的時候。

But, after a few years, she started developing migraines and tremors and feeling tired again.

但在幾年過後，她開始出現偏頭痛與顫抖的症狀，而且又再度感覺疲累。

I thought that it was never gonna get any better and that it... this was my life from here on out.

我以為那種狀況永遠不可能改善了，而且……我的人生從此以後就會是這個樣子。

And it was very depressing.

那當時讓人非常沮喪。

A light at the end of the tunnel? Honestly, it would be meeting Dr. Rye.

苦難之後的光明？老實說，就是遇見賴伊醫師。

Dr. David Rye is a neurologist at the Emory Sleep Center in Atlanta, Georgia.

大衛．賴伊醫師是喬治亞州亞特蘭大埃默裡睡眠中心的神經學家。

Unlike treating traditional narcolepsy, Dr. Rye realized long-term hypersomnia treatment would require more than just forcing patients to stay awake,

不像治療傳統猝睡症，賴伊醫師理解到長期嗜睡症的治療需要的不只是迫使病患保持清醒，

or as he describes it, "pushing the gas pedal with the parking brake engaged."

或是像他說的：「在拉起手煞車的情況下採油門。」

We have approved drugs for narcolepsy. We have nothing for idiopathic hypersomnia.

我們有猝睡症的核准藥品，卻沒有原發性嗜睡症的藥品。

Most patients get treated the same way, because those are the drugs that... medications that help keep a narcoleptic from falling asleep.

大多數的病患都用同樣的方式治療，因為那些藥……那些藥品能夠讓猝睡症患者不至於睡著。

After years of research and testing,

經過多年的研究與試驗，

Dr. Rye and his team found that the brain puts itself to sleep by naturally producing an excess chemical that helps us sleep.

賴伊醫師和他的團隊發現，大腦藉由自然大量分泌一種幫助我們睡眠的化學物質而使其本身睡著。

For hypersomniacs, that chemical never clears away, causing the constant fogginess and the urge to sleep.

在嗜睡症患者身上，這種化學物質從來不會消散，而導致持續不斷的神智迷濛與昏昏欲睡。

In Danielle's case, the stimulants she was relying on to stay awake were wearing off. She was getting desperate.

在丹妮爾這個案例中，她賴以保持清醒的興奮劑已經效果減退。她就要無計可施了。

But, Dr. Rye discovered one more option -- flumazenil.

不過，賴伊醫師發現了另一個選擇——氟馬西尼。

It's a drug sometimes used to help bring people out of anesthesia.

這種藥物有時候會用來幫助讓人從麻醉中清醒過來。

Two years ago, Danielle became only the second person in the United States to get the drug for hypersomnia.

兩年前，丹妮爾成了美國第二個接受這種藥物治療嗜睡症的病患。

Instantly, it was a game changer.

很快地，這種藥物讓她的狀況大為改善。

Within 15 minutes of placing the tablets under my tongue, all of a sudden that wall that I felt like I lived with in my brain, you know, my entire life, was just gone.

把那種藥片放在舌頭底下不到十五分鐘，突然間，我覺得這輩子一直在我腦中擋住我的那道牆就這麼消失了。

It crumbled, and I was able to think clearly and communicate and talk and hold a conversation with somebody without having to look at them.

那道牆粉碎無蹤，我於是可以清晰思考，可以溝通說話，可以在不必看著對方的情況下與人交談。

It was incredible. It actually made me feel, for the first time, like I was alive.

實在是難以置信。這種藥物讓我第一次覺得自己活了起來。
